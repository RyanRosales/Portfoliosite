UX Case Study

CAREERIN

End-to-end Product Design

Overview

I particpated in a hackathon and collaborated with a cross-functional team of four UX Designers and two developers. We had the opportunity to learn about Careerin, a new tech startup, who's vision is to make job searching easier and connect rising talent to the right recruiters.

The Problem

• More and more people are transitioning into the tech industry and landing a job is getting more difficult. How can Careerin help job seekers stand out in the market and find more interviews and job offers?

Project Constraints

• We were expected to present an end-to-end product design in a 3 week period.
• Participants with other priorities could only offer so much of their time to this event.
• The team I worked with experienced communication issues.
• Some participants resigned from the team in the middle and near the end of the hackathon.

  • Timeline: 3 weeks (February, 2021)

  • Role: Research, ideation, prototype, hand-off

  • Software: Miro, Figma

RESEARCH

Careerin's founder, Kenneth Ip, has done tech recruitment for over 10 years and he developed this platform to help job seekers in tech expedite their search for work opportunities. He claimed that recruiters can't "see" a candidate's personality and believes personality is the main reason why many tech professionals struggle to find work, even with a polished resume.

His vision for Careerin is for users to create their own profiles that show who they are outside of their professions and allow 1:1 engagement with recruiters.

Interview with Kenneth Ip.


Research Findings

We conducted four interviews and a Google survey to reach a wider audience. Feedback from our responders revealed 55.3% saw value in showing personality, but felt it can't make up for their lack of work experience.


After we presented our findings to Kenneth, we worked with him to re-design the current sign-up process on the Careerin website.

When users sign up, they are brought to a messaging page that prompts them to connect with Kenneth.


We developed a sitemap with the new product idea for Careerin.

DISCONNECTIONS

After we structured a new user flow, we decided to split the design work evenly. However, members one and two didn't correspond to the user flow and style guide we all agreed on. As a result, we had to restart at the beginning with only a few days left of the hackathon. Unfortunately, they stopped communicating with the rest of the team and formed their own group to close out their portion of the work.

DESIGN REVISIONS

Unhappy with the outcome of the product, another teammate and I reached out to a hackathon coordinator and explained our situation. He directed us to revise the product and contact the team members that left.

We managed to get a hold of the two team members that left and convinced them to have a meeting with the coordinator as a team. He encouraged us to work together again as a team and move forward in this new direction.


Traditional sign-up process.


Users can request endorsements from past co-workers and colleagues through email.

KEY TASK FLOWS

Sign up

Instead of connecting with Kenneth, users can go through the traditional sign up process.



Request Endorsement

Users can request endorsements from previous co-workers and colleagues by sending emails from their profile dashboard.



Endorser

Careerin visitors providing endorsements can only access a user profile through an external link and will be able to create an endorsement video.



Test Results

We had eight testers for our prototypes and five testers were able to navigate with no issues. We took in account the feedback from the remaining three who had to "think twice" to further improve the product.


RESULTS & LEARNINGS

While the product is under development, members one and two suddenly resigned near the end of the hackathon. As a result, member three and I closed out the event with only our names listed. Overall, This hackathon leveled up my collaboration experience and other soft skills. The results weren't ideal, but I resolved to remain composed and patient and tried encouraging my fellow teammates to stay together and focus on shipping out the product.

MY UX CAREER GOALS
I think that no matter what design path I take, user-centered design will always align to my goals: help businesses leverage the experiences of users to shape the success of digital products.

Recently, I took great interest in Extended Reality (XR). As I learned more about these technologies and how it can positively improve the way people live, I became captivated. Since I'm a fan of anything related to the fantasy genre, I saw a connection between fantasy and AR/VR/MR as it introduces a level of immersion that would allow people to visit and experience other "worlds."

While I've tried out VR headsets and Pokemon Go in the past, I never truly saw the beauty of immersive experiences until I recently bought an Oculus Rift S and actually sat down to fully experience it. Honestly, I wish I realized it sooner. Now, I'm looking for the next opportunity as a Product Designer to design immersive XR experiences that could revolutionize healthcare, educational training, daily life and help create the first Full-Dive video game!
DIVING INTO XR
After I bought an Oculus Rift S, my daily activities shifted. I find myself in what I call my "VR office," where I design no-code prototypes in VR tools such as Microsoft Maquette and Sketchbox 3D. Since I'm actively learning more about XR, I've been attending webinar events hosted by Unity and other XR groups that I find on Meetup. Also, I'm expanding my XR knowledge and expertise in Unity and C#. Currently, I'm learning how to develop my first functional AR and VR prototypes!
VIBING IN VRCHAT
I wish I got into VRchat sooner. VRchat is a social game where users can meet other people around the world in virtual reality. I find this a great alternative to in-person meetups. So far, my experience has been great and I've met some cool people.
FORMER COMPETITIVE GAMER WHO TURNED TO THE GACHA LIFE
I used to be a hardcore gamer where I would spend hours playing video games, but after some time, I gradually played less and less. I still game, but I moved my interests towards RPG gacha games. When I began playing gachas in 2017, I found it to be a stress reliever whenever I needed a break from design projects. I would spend around 30 minutes to an hour grinding out daily missions before going back to work.

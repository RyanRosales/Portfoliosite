Background

I am currently a student attending the California State University, Fullerton with an expected graduation date of Spring 2020, with a Bachelor’s Degree in Graphic and Interactive Design.

From October 2019 to January 2020, I was a Visual Design intern for Infinite Peripherals, in Irvine, California. I led the visual design directions for Infinite Peripheral's product pages and translated my designs into coding language to achieve effective communication with the development team. In addition, I used my skills in Graphic Design and Typography to produce social media and print content; as well as assisting the Creative Director with the production of product motion graphics. Finally, I was given the opportunity to participate in Infinite Peripheral's vsual marketing for NRF 2020.

Working for Infinite Peripherals has granted me opportunities to grow my design skills and learn business and marketing values. Overall, my hunger for knowledge and my desire to grow as an individual allowed me to adapt in a high-pressure environment from completing multiple projects on a time crunch and handling last-minute changes.

I am obsessed with my craft and I actively research and cultivate my design skills daily. I try to make it a habit of thinking “UX” in my daily life and I express it with any person I interact with. I believe this habit reflects my desire to create meaningful relationships from the interactions I have with other people.

VR ARCHERY

Learning Unity and VR Design

Overview

A self-study for learning Unity, the basics of C# programming, and environment design.

  • Project: Solo

  • Timeline: 2 weeks (February, 2021)

  • Role: UX/UI, 3D

  • Software: Unity

CODING A BOW AND ARROW

With no background in C# programming, attempting to learn the language felt intimidating, but it didn't stop my curiousity of trying to understand it.

I referenced a tutorial series to help me setup the bow and arrow interactions. However, since Unity updates frequently, the tutorial became outdated, resulting in a handful of code errors in the latest versions. I eventually debugged the console errors by referencing Unity's documentations and various YouTube tutorials.

USABILITY TESTING

I tested my prototype's functionality with two people. Their feedback mentioned the bow and arrow felt unnatural. When held, the bow floated outside the hand model and the tutorial only set up the quiver to float in space.

REPLICATING REALISTIC ARCHERY

Bow

I looked into the bow inspector in Unity and I discovered the position of the XRGrabInteractor was outside of the bow model. I re-positioned the game object so it would sit within the hand model when picked up.

Arrow

Instead of making users reach out into space to grab floating arrows, I attached the quiver to the main Camera Rig. Now, users can physically reach over their shoulder to grab an arrow.

ENVIRONMENT DESIGN

After solving the interaction problems, I designed various environments in Unity, beginning with a forest to the current Sci-fi industrial theme.

WHAT I LEARNED

This project was an amazing hands-on experience to begin VR Design. I was able to familiarize myself in Unity and learn C# concepts. In addition, I learned that people treat VR as if they're in the real world. It's very interesting to see that although people in VR know they're in a immersive digital experience, they naturally perform instinctive actions.

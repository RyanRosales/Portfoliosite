CRUNCHYROLL
A redesign of a leading Anime streaming platform where I modernized the UI and created a solution to organize saved Anime playlists.
Disclaimer: I am not an employee nor associated with Crunchyroll. Just a designer who loves Anime.
DESIGN ROLE
I was the sole UX/UI Designer where I identified current UX problems that affect Crunchyroll’s business goals, I was able to drive the design solutions based on research and user needs.

Project Date: July, 2020 - August, 2020

Tools: Figma, Affinity Designer, Photoshop, Draw.io

Research

WHAT IS CRUNCHYROLL AND ANIME?
Crunchyroll is an American video streaming platform that focuses on licensed Japanese Anime and live-action drama. Anime is short for Japanese animation which is known for its 2-D and stylized art style. In addition, Anime is heavily a part of Japanese culture and has a growing international audience each year.

With over 70 million registered users (updated July, 2020), 13 million monthly active users (updated March, 2019), and 3 million paying subscribers (updated July, 2020), Crunchyroll is without a doubt the leading Anime streaming service.
IDENTIFYING CRUNCHYROLL'S BUSINESS GOALS
Crunchyroll’s main forms of revenue:

• Premium Subscription allows users to watch Anime without ads and watch new episodes 1-hour after Japanese airing time.

• Online Store is where users can purchase Crunchyroll merchandise and other goods.

Crunchyroll’s methods of user conversion:

• Anime, Manga, and Drama content

• SEO strategies

• News page

• Mobile games

I conducted an online survey with 11 responses that had a question with the intent on finding out if these users use any other website aside from Crunchyroll to watch Anime. 18.2% also use Funimation, 81.8% also use Netflix, 36.4% also use Hulu, and three of the responders also use YouTube, KissAnime, and Animelab.
COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS


Along with research on Crunchyroll’s business, I dug deeper to understand the UX problems. In my survey, I asked the users how they felt about the current design layout, navigation experience, what pages they use the most and the least, and any other issues they may have on the website. While the answers were mixed, feedback requested:

• A UI update, especially the landing page and shows page.

• Dark mode.

• A way to organize their queue/watch lists.

• Filter options to improve the search engine.

The feedback showed that the users who addressed their opinions towards the outdated design gave them a negative user experience overall and the desire to have a way to organize their saved Anime into different playlists.

IDENTIFYING WHAT PAGES TO REDESIGN
Landing page - My solution for this was to give Crunchyroll’s pages a spotlight of their own through the landing page. My goal was to improve user conversion and increase revenue.



Shows Page - Users should have the freedom to make their searches as quick and painless as possible. Crunchyroll does have a filtration system for their search engine, but I believe it could be improved as the current navigation can be confusing.



Queue List - User feedback mentioned that favorited Anime is listed on the last viewed episode. This sparks confusion because many Anime have multiple seasons.

I tested this myself with an Anime that I know that currently has 5 seasons. I previewed a random episode from a random season and checked my queue list. It read as “Shokugeki no Soma — Episode 5 *title name*.” My immediate thought was “Episode 5 from which season?”



Anime Info Page - Feedback I received when a user visits the page of an Anime show is to make current shows more apparent on its specific page and on the front page. The current layout is not visually engaging so I kept in my mind to modernize it and have the user feel they are paying for high quality content.

PERSONA
I created a persona based on the results of my user research. In addition, I implemented other Crunchyroll statistics that I found while conducting my research; which drove my persona to be a 22-year old Asian male who is a college student.

• 75% of subscribers are under the age of 35 (updated January 2016).

• Median age of free users: 18 years old (updated January 2016).

Design

WIREFRAME AND A/B TESTING
I jumped into wireframing and through multiple iterations, I needed feedback on ideas that seemed the most efficient in improving user conversion and the user experience. I shared my solutions to my Instagram followers for their insight.

USABILITY TESTING
Figma Prototype

I took 4 Crunchyroll users and had them test my prototype.

Task Flow

• Start on the home page.

• Navigate to the “Shows” page.

• Add an Anime from the “Shows” page to the Watch list.

• Navigate to the watch list page and add that Anime to a specific playlist.

• Finally, visit the page of that Anime.

Results

100% OF THE TESTERS LIKED THE PROPOSED SOLUTIONS, ESPECIALLY THE CONTENT FILTERS I ADDED ON THE SHOWS AND WATCH LIST PAGES.

MOVING FORWARD WITH THE DESIGN SOLUTIONS
Storytelling - By updating the design into a storytelling layout, and giving off Netflix and Hulu vibes, the landing page is more visually engaging and each Crunchyroll page: Shows, Manga, News, Store, and Games are highlighted. I believe this approach would increase revenue and user conversion due to all Crunchyroll services being much more visible and clear call-to-actions to subscribe to their premium service.



Shows page - User feedback desired dark mode as many commented about eye strain. In addition, I added more white space so that the Anime listings has breathing room and can be processed more easily. I reduced the filtration system to Trending, Seasonal, Drama, and Genres as filters such as “alphabetical,” are confusing and should be implemented by default. Finally, I included a sorting filter to optimize the search engine.



Watch List - I personally felt that not everyone will understand what a “queue list” is, so I decided to change the name to “Watch list,” to give a bit more clarity on what this page does.

Users can see what Anime are currently airing and options to organize their content. They have the freedom to create multiple playlists, which can help organize content for users, especially those who happen to share their account with multiple people. Finally, I added playlist and genre filters for quick and easy navigation.



Show Info Page - Is now more visually engaging by making the video preview much larger and more apparent. In terms of functionality, when a user enters the page of an Anime series, their progress is saved according to the last episode they watched. So when they return at a later time they can quickly continue where they left off.



Responsive Design - Finally, I made a responsive layout as the current website is not responsive.

Conclusion

FINAL THOUGHTS
I had a great time doing this redesign and I’m proud of the results. If I could go back, I would do more usability testing to maximize feedback on the proposed solutions.

Overall, I definitely think that the results from this project could be one way to improve Crunchyroll’s user experience by giving it a visual update that optimizes content filters, helps improve navigational experience and user conversion, and increase revenue.
